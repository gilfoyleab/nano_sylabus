# Goals and Metrics

## Business goals

The business goals derived from the BRD are:

1. Acquire 1,000 student users within 6 months of launch.
2. Reach sustainable revenue through tiered subscriptions.
3. Build defensible Nepal-specific syllabus coverage.
4. Create admin tooling that keeps the product current as syllabi change.
5. Retain students through ongoing study value, not one-time curiosity.

## Product goals

The product goals for V1 and early growth are:

1. Deliver a chat experience that feels useful for Nepali students immediately.
2. Make Roman Nepali a real first-class experience.
3. Personalize answers using academic profile context.
4. Convert good answers into notes and revision workflows.
5. Make credit usage and plan upgrades understandable and predictable.

## Success metrics

### BRD targets

| Metric | 6 months | 12 months |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Registered users | 1,000 | 3,000 |
| Paid subscribers | 300 | 600 |
| Monthly active users | 30% of registered | 40% of registered |
| Average session length | 12 minutes | 18 minutes |
| Support tickets per user | < 0.1 / month | < 0.05 / month |
| NPS | > 40 | > 55 |

## Operational KPIs we should track

### Acquisition

- landing page to signup conversion
- signup completion rate
- onboarding completion rate
- cost per acquired student

### Activation

- first chat started
- first successful AI response
- first note saved
- first revision session started

### Retention

- day 1, day 7, day 30 retention
- weekly active users
- number of sessions per active student
- notes saved per active student
- revision sessions per active student

### Monetization

- free to paid conversion
- MRR
- plan distribution
- average revenue per paying user
- credit exhaustion to upgrade conversion

### Quality and trust

- thumbs-down rate on AI responses
- support tickets per active user
- flagged-answer rate
- retrieval quality and hallucination complaints

## Near-term execution goals for this repo

Because the current repository is still a prototype, the immediate goals are more practical:

1. Convert the prototype into a real student product foundation.
2. Replace local mock data with persistent backend-backed data.
3. connect chat to a real AI pipeline with syllabus grounding.
4. implement subscription and invoice flows properly.
5. establish a separate admin surface and content operations workflow.

## Non-goals for the immediate next phase

- native mobile apps
- teacher portal
- parent portal
- live tutoring
- audio/video generation

Those may matter later, but they should not distract the current build.
